California Pavement Contractors: Compare the Scope Before the Price

For property managers, the biggest paving risk is not hiring the most expensive contractor. It is approving the cheapest bid without seeing what was left out.

Decode your bidsGet a condition report

California paving projects are shaped by labor cost, material volatility, ADA exposure, traffic-control requirements, weather windows, and site access. A clean-looking bid may still omit base repair, drainage correction, tack coat, ADA responsibility, striping details, or warranty exclusions that change the real cost.

ADA risk is local and expensive

California's Unruh Act makes small accessible-parking misses financially meaningful. Every paving scope should identify ADA impacts before work starts.

Base repair decides lifespan

Surface work placed over failed base can look good at turnover and still fail early. Require quantities, depths, and unit pricing.

Lowest bid is often incomplete

Compare cost per useful year, not just sticker price. A shorter-life scope can be the more expensive decision.

What to Ask Before Hiring a California Pavement Contractor

Ask each contractor to define milling depth, overlay thickness, patching quantities, base repair assumptions, drainage fixes, traffic control, striping, ADA compliance responsibility, and warranty terms. If those line items are missing, you are not comparing equal scopes.
