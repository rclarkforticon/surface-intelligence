// Bay Area pavement planning

Asphalt, concrete, ADA, and bid strategy before the scope is signed.

Surface Intelligence helps property managers, HOA boards, facility teams, and commercial owners make better pavement decisions before capital dollars move. The free tools are built around the questions that come up on real Bay Area projects: whether to repair or replace, how to compare contractor bids, when sealcoat is actually appropriate, what ADA parking issues should be documented, and how to explain pavement work to owners or board members.

Most parking lot mistakes start with an unclear scope. A bid can look competitive while leaving out base repair, drainage correction, concrete transitions, ADA responsibility, striping details, traffic control, or warranty exclusions. That is why Surface Intelligence focuses on practical decision support rather than generic paving advice. Use the Three-Bid Decoder to compare cost per useful year, the Paving Project Budget Estimator to set a realistic range, and the ADA Risk Scorecard to identify accessible parking red flags before resurfacing begins.

Local guidance for Bay Area commercial properties

Forticon serves commercial, industrial, HOA, multifamily, school, church, and retail properties across the Bay Area. Surface Intelligence supports that work with educational resources for Alameda, Oakland, San Leandro, Berkeley, Hayward, Walnut Creek, Lafayette, San Mateo, Fremont, San Francisco, and nearby service areas. City pages include long-tail topics such as San Leandro paving contractor comparisons, Lafayette ADA contractor questions, Walnut Creek concrete sidewalk replacement, Fremont industrial paving, and Bay Area parking lot maintenance planning.
