Wondering if your parking lot is an ADA target?

If you came from a post about ADA parking lawsuits, start with a practical first-pass check. The goal is not to replace a formal survey. It is to spot obvious exposure before a complaint, resurfacing project, or restriping job makes the issue urgent.

Resurfacing can expose old ADA problems.

When striping, paving, or concrete work starts, accessible parking, route slopes, signage, and access aisles deserve a closer look.

// What to check

Four things to document before work begins.

Stalls

Accessible stall count and layout

Check stall count, van-accessible spaces, access aisles, striping contrast, and whether the layout still matches current needs.

Routes

Path from parking to entrance

Look at route continuity, transitions, curb ramps, abrupt changes, obstructions, drainage, and obvious slope concerns.

Scope

What the paving bid includes

Do not assume resurfacing, sealcoat, or striping automatically solves compliance. Make the ADA scope explicit before approval.
