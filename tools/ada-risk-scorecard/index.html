▎Common questions

What triggers an ADA parking lot inspection?

A complaint from a disabled person, a demand letter from an ADA plaintiff attorney, a permit application for renovation or construction, or a routine compliance sweep by a serial litigant's investigator. California has some of the most active ADA serial litigation environments in the country. Properties that haven't been evaluated in several years are at elevated risk, especially if paving or striping work has been done recently.

What are the most common ADA parking lot violations?

Missing or faded van-accessible signage, access aisles that are too narrow or blocked, slopes that exceed 2% in accessible stalls or aisles, missing or incorrect "tow-away" sign language, path-of-travel obstructions like cracked pavement or raised lips at transitions, and insufficient number of accessible stalls for the lot size. All 8 are covered in this tool.

Does repaving trigger ADA compliance requirements?

Yes — under the ADA, "alterations" to an area of a facility must bring that area into compliance to the maximum extent feasible. Repaving a parking lot can constitute an alteration, which may trigger requirements to upgrade accessible stalls, access aisles, signage, and path-of-travel routes. This is one of the most overlooked liability triggers in commercial paving projects.
