What does your rating mean?

GOOD

Pavement is in solid condition. Invest in preventive maintenance (seal coat, crack fill) to extend life. You have time to budget for major work.

FAIR

Visible aging. Start planning for overlay or mill-and-pave in the next 2-3 years. Get contractor proposals to understand scope and cost.

POOR

Significant deterioration. Plan for mill-and-pave or full replacement within 12-24 months. Get bids now so you can budget accurately.

CRITICAL

Immediate action needed. Your pavement is failing and creating liability risk. Get contractor bids ASAP and prioritize this in your budget.

📌 These ratings are visual assessments only. For engineering-level analysis, consult a pavement engineer or your contractor.

▎Common questions

What is a Pavement Condition Index (PCI)?

The Pavement Condition Index is a standardized 0–100 scale used to rate the structural and functional integrity of a pavement surface. A score of 100 is new pavement; below 40 typically signals major distress requiring significant rehabilitation or reconstruction. This tool provides a simplified first-pass estimate — not an ASTM D6433-certified assessment.

When should a property manager get a pavement condition assessment?

Before any capital budget request, before soliciting paving bids, and every 3–5 years as a baseline. A condition assessment gives you defensible documentation for board presentations, insurance claims, and ADA compliance discussions. Use this tool to get a rough read — then call a specialist if the score is in the moderate-to-poor range.

What PCI score requires immediate repair?

Generally, a PCI below 40 indicates significant structural distress requiring either major rehabilitation or reconstruction. Scores between 40 and 60 often call for preventive treatment or targeted repairs. Above 70, routine maintenance like crack sealing or sealcoating is typically sufficient. This tool maps your answers to a similar scale with an actionable recommendation.
